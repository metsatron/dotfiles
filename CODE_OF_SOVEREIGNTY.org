* Code of Sovereignty
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: code-of-sovereignty
:END:

This project is governed by the *Code of Sovereignty* — a statement of
maintainer conscience, meritocratic governance, and the right of refusal.

It replaces any Code of Conduct. It is not a compromise with one. It is
a philosophically distinct instrument grounded in the traditions of free
software, individual liberty, and the sovereignty of the craftsman over
their work.

** Preamble
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: preamble
:END:

Free software was born from a simple principle: the user and the
developer are sovereign over the machines they operate and the code they
write. This sovereignty is not granted by institutions. It is inherent.

The same principle that protects the four freedoms — to run, study,
modify, and distribute software — also protects the freedom of
conscience of every contributor and maintainer. No project governance
document may legitimately require ideological conformity as a condition
of participation, any more than a license may restrict the field of
endeavour in which software is used.

This Code of Sovereignty affirms these inherited freedoms and extends
them to the full scope of project governance.

** Article I — Merit as the Sole Criterion
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: article-i
:END:

1. Contributions to this project are evaluated on *technical merit*
   alone: correctness, clarity, performance, maintainability, and
   alignment with the project's stated objectives.

2. The identity, beliefs, affiliations, demographics, or personal
   characteristics of a contributor are irrelevant to the evaluation of
   their work. Code has no identity. Patches have no politics.

3. No contributor shall receive preferential or adverse treatment on any
   basis other than the quality and relevance of their contribution.

4. The maintainer reserves the sole right to accept, reject, or request
   changes to any contribution, for any technical or project-alignment
   reason, without obligation to justify beyond the technical.

** Article II — Freedom of Conscience
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: article-ii
:END:

1. No participant in this project shall be required to affirm, endorse,
   or perform agreement with any belief system, political position,
   social theory, or ideological framework as a condition of
   participation.

2. No participant shall be penalized for holding, expressing, or
   declining to express any belief, provided their conduct within the
   project remains relevant to the project's technical objectives.

3. Compelled speech — including but not limited to mandatory
   affirmations, pledges, pronoun declarations, or ideological
   statements — is incompatible with freedom of conscience and shall not
   be imposed.

4. This project acknowledges that contributors hold diverse and
   sometimes irreconcilable worldviews. The project's function is to
   produce excellent software, not to adjudicate philosophical disputes.

** Article III — Right of Association and Refusal
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: article-iii
:END:

1. The maintainer retains the unrestricted right to define the scope,
   direction, and boundaries of this project.

2. The maintainer retains the right to refuse contributions, close
   discussions, or remove participants whose conduct disrupts the
   project's technical mission — including, but not limited to, attempts
   to redirect the project toward objectives unrelated to its stated
   purpose.

3. No external organization, foundation, working group, or collective
   may impose governance requirements upon this project without the
   explicit, freely given, written consent of the maintainer.

4. Participation in this project is voluntary. Those who find its
   governance principles incompatible with their values are free to
   fork the project under the terms of its license. The right to fork
   is sacred to free software and is honoured without reservation.

** Article IV — Rejection of Ideological Capture
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: article-iv
:END:

1. This project rejects all forms of institutional ideological capture
   — the process by which organizations, governance documents, or
   community norms are co-opted to serve political ends unrelated to
   the project's technical mission.

2. Specifically, this project will not adopt any governance document
   that:
   - Defines acceptable or unacceptable *beliefs* (as distinct from
     *conduct*);
   - Establishes identity categories as relevant to contribution
     evaluation;
   - Creates adjudication mechanisms for disputes unrelated to the
     project's technical scope;
   - Requires affirmation of contested social, political, or
     philosophical positions;
   - Delegates enforcement authority to external organizations.

3. The maintainer recognizes that many widely adopted Codes of Conduct
   function as instruments of ideological conformity rather than
   genuine community protection. This Code of Sovereignty exists as a
   principled alternative.

4. This position is not hostility toward any person or group. It is
   the defence of a boundary: the boundary between a technical project
   and a political project. This project is the former.

** Article V — Conduct Within the Project
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: article-v
:END:

1. All interactions within this project's issue trackers, pull
   requests, mailing lists, and communication channels shall remain
   *relevant to the project's technical objectives*.

2. Personal attacks, harassment, doxxing, and threats are unacceptable
   — not because an external document mandates this, but because they
   are incompatible with productive technical collaboration and basic
   human decency.

3. Disagreements about technical decisions are welcomed and expected.
   They shall be resolved on technical grounds: evidence, benchmarks,
   code review, and reasoned argument.

4. Discussions unrelated to the project's technical scope —
   including political, ideological, or social advocacy — are
   off-topic and may be closed or removed at the maintainer's
   discretion. This is not censorship; it is scope management.

5. If something is illegal in the maintainer's jurisdiction, it is
   not permitted within the project. This Code does not override
   governing law.

** Article VI — The Maintainer's Vocation
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: article-vi
:END:

1. The maintainer of this project regards software craftsmanship as a
   vocation — work done with care, intention, and integrity, offered
   freely but not owed to anyone.

2. Free software is a gift economy. The maintainer gives freely of
   their labour. In return, the maintainer owes nothing: not
   responsiveness, not accommodation, not emotional labour, not
   ideological compliance.

3. The maintainer's right to set boundaries around their own labour,
   attention, and creative output is absolute. Burnout in the service
   of others' demands is not virtue; it is self-destruction.

4. Users and contributors who approach this project with respect for
   the craft and the craftsman will find a generous collaborator.
   Those who approach with entitlement, coercion, or bad faith will
   find a closed door.

** Article VII — On Licensing and Downstream Use
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: article-vii
:END:

1. This project's code is licensed under the terms specified in the
   LICENSE file. Those terms define the legal rights and obligations of
   all parties.

2. No governance document — including this one — may restrict the
   freedoms granted by a free software license. If there is any
   conflict between this Code of Sovereignty and the project's
   license, the license prevails.

3. Downstream forks may adopt any governance model they choose. The
   right to fork includes the right to govern differently. This is by
   design.

4. The maintainer will not impose ideological conditions on downstream
   use beyond what the license permits. Software freedom is
   indivisible.

** Article VIII — Amendments
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: article-viii
:END:

1. This Code of Sovereignty may be amended only by the maintainer.

2. Amendments shall be version-controlled and documented in the
   project's commit history.

3. No amendment shall contradict the core principles of this document:
   meritocratic evaluation, freedom of conscience, rejection of
   ideological capture, and the sovereignty of the maintainer over
   their project.

** Closing
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: closing
:END:

Participation in this project constitutes agreement with this Code of
Sovereignty. If you cannot work within these principles, you are free to
fork the project and govern it as you see fit. That freedom is the
foundation upon which all of this rests.
